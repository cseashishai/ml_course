India, located in South Asia, is the world's most populous country (over 1.4 billion) and the 7th largest by area, boasting a rich, ancient civilization. It is a diverse parliamentary republic with 28 states, 8 union territories, and a vibrant, multi-ethnic, and multi-religious society. Major highlights include its Himalayan border, booming tech/automobile industries, and vegetarian, spice-rich cuisine.

Key Aspects of India
Geography: Bounded by the Himalayas in the north and surrounded by the Indian Ocean, Arabian Sea, and Bay of Bengal. It has a tropical climate, with intense summers and a significant monsoon season.
Government & Politics: A sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic with a parliamentary system (est. 1950). It is the world's largest democracy, led by a President (Head of State) and Prime Minister (Head of Government).
Culture & Society: Known for extreme diversity in languages, religions, and traditions. Hinduism is the majority religion, but it is a secular nation with significant Muslim, Christian, Sikh, and other communities.
Economy & Tech: Features one of the fastest-growing economies, with a major emphasis on technology, software exports, and engineering. It boasts the world's third-largest higher education system.
Interesting Facts:
Oldest Civilization: Home to the ancient Indus Valley civilization.
Population: Became the most populous country in 2023.
Cuisine: Highest percentage of vegetarians globally (around 38%).
Innovations: Contributed the game of 'Snakes and Ladders' to the world.
Symbols: The National Flag is a tricolour of saffron, white, and green with the Ashoka Chakra; the National Emblem is the Lion Capital of Ashoka.
